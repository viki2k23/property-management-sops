# Rent Follow-Up Communication Templates

**Document type:** Communication Templates  
**Category:** Tenant Communication — Rent Collection  
**Applies to:** Property managers, operations coordinators, leasing support staff  
**Last updated:** April 2026  
**Author:** Vicky | Property Management Operations  

---

## Purpose

This document provides a 3-stage rent follow-up communication sequence for
handling late or missing rent payments. Templates are designed to be
professional, firm, legally cautious, and easy to adapt across different
tenants and situations.

Each stage escalates in urgency while maintaining a tone that protects the
landlord-tenant relationship where possible and creates a clear paper trail
where necessary.

---

## Important Disclaimer

These templates are for communication purposes only. They do not constitute
legal notices. For formal legal notices such as Pay or Quit, Notice to Vacate,
or eviction filings, consult a licensed attorney or follow your state's
specific landlord-tenant statutes.

---

## When to Use Each Stage

| Stage | Trigger | Tone |
|---|---|---|
| Stage 1 — Friendly Reminder | Rent is 1–3 days past due | Warm, assumes oversight |
| Stage 2 — Firm Follow-Up | No response after Stage 1, rent is 5–7 days past due | Firm, professional, deadline-driven |
| Stage 3 — Final Notice | No payment or response, rent is 10–14 days past due | Formal, factual, escalation implied |

---

## Stage 1 — Friendly Reminder (Day 1–3 Past Due)

**Use when:** Rent has not been received and the tenant has not reached out.
Assume it may be an oversight before assuming non-payment.

**Preferred channel:** Text message or email  
**Follow up with:** Phone call if no response within 24 hours

---

### Template 1A — Text Message

> Hi [Tenant First Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company Name].
> Just a quick reminder that your rent of $[Amount] for [Month] was due on
> [Due Date]. If you've already submitted payment, please disregard this
> message. If not, please arrange payment at your earliest convenience.
> Thank you.

---

### Template 1B — Email

**Subject:** Rent Payment Reminder — [Unit Address] — [Month]

> Hi [Tenant Name],
>
> I hope you are doing well. I wanted to send a quick reminder that your
> rent payment of $[Amount] for [Month] was due on [Due Date] and has not
> yet been reflected in our system.
>
> If you have already submitted payment through [portal name or payment
> method], it may still be processing — please feel free to send over a
> confirmation so we can update our records.
>
> If payment has not yet been made, please arrange it as soon as possible
> to avoid a late fee being applied to your account.
>
> Late fee policy: A late fee of $[Amount] is applied after [X] days per
> your lease agreement (Section [X]).
>
> Please don't hesitate to reach out if you have any questions or if you're
> experiencing a situation you'd like to discuss.
>
> Thank you,  
> [Your Name]  
> [Company Name] — Property Management  
> [Phone] | [Email]

---

## Stage 2 — Firm Follow-Up (Day 5–7 Past Due)

**Use when:** Stage 1 message was sent with no response or payment received.
The tone shifts to professional urgency. A specific deadline is introduced.

**Preferred channel:** Email (primary) + text message (secondary)  
**Document:** Log date, time, and method of all outreach attempts

---

### Template 2A — Text Message

> Hi [Tenant First Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company Name].
> Your rent of $[Amount] for [Month] remains outstanding as of today,
> [Date]. Please make payment by [Deadline Date] to avoid further action
> on your account. If you are experiencing a hardship, please contact us
> today to discuss options. Thank you.

---

### Template 2B — Email

**Subject:** Outstanding Rent Balance — Action Required — [Unit Address]

> Hi [Tenant Name],
>
> This is a follow-up regarding the unpaid rent balance on your account.
>
> **Account Summary:**
> - Unit: [Full Property Address, Unit #]
> - Tenant: [Tenant Full Name]
> - Rent due: $[Base Rent Amount]
> - Late fee (if applicable): $[Late Fee Amount]
> - Total balance due: $[Total]
> - Original due date: [Due Date]
> - Payment deadline: [Firm Deadline — typically 3 days from this notice]
>
> We have not received payment or a response to our previous message sent
> on [Date of Stage 1 Contact].
>
> **Please submit full payment by [Deadline Date] through [payment method
> or portal link].**
>
> If you are facing a financial hardship or an unexpected situation, we
> encourage you to contact us before this deadline so we can discuss
> available options. We prefer to resolve this directly.
>
> Failure to pay or respond by [Deadline Date] will require us to take
> further steps per the terms of your lease agreement and applicable
> state law.
>
> This message serves as formal written notice of your outstanding balance.
>
> Sincerely,  
> [Your Name]  
> [Title]  
> [Company Name] — Property Management  
> [Phone] | [Email]

---

## Stage 3 — Final Notice Before Escalation (Day 10–14 Past Due)

**Use when:** No payment or meaningful communication received after Stages 1
and 2. This notice signals the final step before formal legal action is
initiated. It must be factual, unemotional, and fully documented.

**Preferred channel:** Email with read receipt + certified mail if applicable  
**CC or notify:** Property owner  
**Document:** Screenshot or save all prior communications before sending

> **Important:** In many U.S. states, a formal Pay or Quit Notice must be
> delivered in a legally specific manner (written, posted, or certified mail)
> before an eviction can be filed. This template is a pre-legal communication
> only. Consult your state's landlord-tenant law or a licensed attorney before
> proceeding to formal notice.

---

### Template 3A — Final Notice Email

**Subject:** Final Notice — Unpaid Rent — [Unit Address] — [Month]

> Dear [Tenant Full Name],
>
> This is a final written notice regarding the unpaid rent on your account.
>
> **Outstanding balance summary:**
> - Property address: [Full Address, Unit #]
> - Lease holder: [Tenant Full Name]
> - Rent amount: $[Amount]
> - Late fees: $[Amount]
> - Total amount due: $[Total]
> - Days past due: [Number]
> - Payment deadline: [Final Deadline Date]
>
> Despite previous notices sent on [Date of Stage 1] and [Date of Stage 2],
> we have not received payment or a satisfactory response.
>
> **You are required to submit full payment of $[Total] by [Final Deadline
> Date].** Payment must be made through [accepted payment method].
>
> If full payment is not received by [Final Deadline Date], we will have no
> choice but to proceed with the next steps available under your lease
> agreement and [State] landlord-tenant law, which may include issuance of
> a formal Pay or Quit Notice and initiation of eviction proceedings.
>
> If you believe this notice has been sent in error, or if you have already
> submitted payment, please contact us immediately with documentation.
>
> This communication is being retained as part of your tenant file.
>
> Sincerely,  
> [Your Name]  
> [Title]  
> [Company Name] — Property Management  
> [Phone] | [Email]  
>
> cc: [Property Owner Name, if applicable]

## Phone Call Script — All Stages

When calling a tenant about an unpaid balance, use this structure:

**Opening:**
> "Hi, may I speak with [Tenant Name]? This is [Your Name] calling from
> [Company Name] regarding your rental account at [Address]."

**If tenant answers:**
> "I'm reaching out because we have an outstanding rent balance of $[Amount]
> for [Month] that was due on [Due Date]. I wanted to connect with you
> directly before taking any further steps. Can you give me an update on
> when we can expect payment?"

**If voicemail:**
> "Hi [Tenant Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company Name] calling
> regarding your account at [Address]. We have an outstanding balance of
> $[Amount] for [Month]. Please call us back at [Phone Number] or respond
> to our email at your earliest convenience. Thank you."

**After every call — document immediately:**
- Date and time of call
- Who answered or if voicemail was left
- What was said or committed to
- Next follow-up action and date

## Dos and Don'ts

**Do:**
- Always communicate in writing and keep copies
- Stay professional and factual in every message
- Document every contact attempt with date and time
- Notify the property owner before Stage 3
- Reference the lease agreement and section numbers where relevant

**Do not:**
- Threaten illegal actions (illegal lockout, utility shutoff as leverage)
- Use aggressive or emotionally charged language
- Accept verbal payment promises without written confirmation
- Delay Stage 2 or 3 out of personal discomfort — delays weaken your position
- Share tenant account information with unauthorized parties

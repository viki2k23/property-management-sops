# Move-In / Move-Out Inspection Checklist

**Document type:** Checklist + Inspection Template  
**Category:** Leasing Operations  
**Applies to:** Property managers, leasing coordinators, field inspectors  
**Last updated:** April 2026  
**Author:** Vicky | Property Management Operations

---

## Purpose

This checklist establishes a consistent, documented inspection process for
unit move-ins and move-outs. It protects the property owner against
unwarranted security deposit disputes, creates a clear record of unit
condition at each tenancy transition, and sets professional expectations
for both incoming and outgoing tenants.

A completed checklist signed by both parties is one of the most important
documents in a tenancy file.

---

## How to Use This Checklist

1. Complete a separate checklist for move-in and move-out
2. Walk every room in the unit — do not skip areas
3. Photograph every item marked as Damaged or Note
4. Both property manager and tenant sign the completed form
5. Provide tenant a copy at time of signing
6. Retain original in the tenant's file for the duration of tenancy plus
   3 years minimum

---

## Condition Rating Key

| Code | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **G** | Good — clean, functional, no visible damage |
| **F** | Fair — minor wear consistent with normal use |
| **D** | Damaged — visible damage beyond normal wear and tear |
| **N/A** | Not applicable — item does not exist in this unit |
| **Note** | Requires written description in the Notes column |

> **Normal wear and tear** includes minor scuffs, small nail holes from
> picture hanging, and carpet wear from regular foot traffic. It does not
> include stains, burns, broken fixtures, holes in walls, or damage caused
> by pets, negligence, or misuse.

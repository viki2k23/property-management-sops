# Lease Renewal Notice Templates

**Document type:** Communication Templates  
**Category:** Leasing Operations — Lease Renewal  
**Applies to:** Property managers, leasing coordinators, operations support staff  
**Last updated:** April 2026  
**Author:** Vicky | Property Management Operations

---

## Purpose

This document provides ready-to-use templates for managing the full lease
renewal communication sequence. It covers early outreach, formal renewal
offer, rent increase notice, non-renewal notice, and follow-up for
non-responsive tenants.

A proactive, well-timed renewal process reduces vacancy, protects rental
income, and maintains positive tenant relationships. The cost of a vacancy
almost always exceeds the cost of a reasonable concession to retain a
good tenant.

---

## Important Disclaimer

Lease renewal and rent increase notices are subject to state and local
landlord-tenant laws including required notice periods, rent control
ordinances, and just cause requirements. Templates in this document are
for communication purposes only. Always verify compliance with applicable
law in your jurisdiction before sending formal notices.

## Renewal Timeline Overview

| Timeframe Before Lease End | Action |
|---|---|
| 90 days out | Internal review — decide on renewal terms, rent adjustment |
| 60 days out | Send early renewal outreach to tenant (Template 1) |
| 45 days out | Send formal renewal offer with updated terms (Template 2) |
| 30 days out | Follow up if no response received (Template 3) |
| 30 days out | Send non-renewal notice if not renewing (Template 4) |
| 14 days out | Final follow-up for non-responsive tenants (Template 5) |
| Lease end | Confirm signed renewal or begin turnover process |

> **State notice requirements:** Many states require landlords to provide
> 30–60 days written notice of non-renewal or rent increases. Some
> jurisdictions require 90 days or more. Confirm your state's requirement
> before using these templates.

---

## Internal Review Checklist (90 Days Out)

Before contacting the tenant, complete this internal review:

- [ ] Confirm lease end date
- [ ] Review tenant payment history — any late payments or lease violations?
- [ ] Review maintenance history — any unresolved issues or recurring problems?
- [ ] Pull current market rent comps for comparable units in the area
- [ ] Determine new rental rate (increase, hold, or decrease)
- [ ] Confirm owner's renewal preference
- [ ] Decide renewal term offered (12-month, 6-month, month-to-month)
- [ ] Confirm any changes to lease terms (pet policy, utilities, parking, etc.)
- [ ] Set follow-up dates in your calendar for each stage

## Template 1 — Early Renewal Outreach (60 Days Out)

**Purpose:** Open the conversation early. Friendly, low-pressure, gives
tenant time to plan.

**Channel:** Email or letter  
**Tone:** Warm and professional

---

**Subject:** Your Lease is Coming Up — Let's Talk Renewal — [Unit Address]

> Dear [Tenant Full Name],
>
> I hope you are doing well. I am reaching out because your current lease
> at [Full Property Address, Unit #] is scheduled to expire on
> [Lease End Date] — approximately 60 days from now.
>
> We wanted to connect with you early to give you plenty of time to make
> your plans. We value you as a tenant and would love to discuss continuing
> your tenancy at this property.
>
> We are currently preparing renewal options and will follow up within the
> next two weeks with formal details including the proposed lease term and
> updated rental rate.
>
> In the meantime, if you already know that you do not intend to renew,
> please let us know as soon as possible so we can plan accordingly. Per
> your lease agreement, a written notice of [X days — per lease] is
> required if you choose not to renew.
>
> Please feel free to reach out with any questions.
>
> Warm regards,  
> [Your Name]  
> [Title]  
> [Company Name] — Property Management  
> [Phone] | [Email]

## Template 2 — Formal Renewal Offer (45 Days Out)

**Purpose:** Present the official renewal offer with updated terms. Clear,
specific, and deadline-driven.

**Channel:** Email + follow up by mail if required by state law  
**Tone:** Professional and direct

---

**Subject:** Lease Renewal Offer — [Unit Address] — Action Required by [Date]

> Dear [Tenant Full Name],
>
> Thank you for being a resident at [Full Property Address, Unit #]. As
> your lease expires on [Lease End Date], we are pleased to offer you the
> opportunity to renew your tenancy under the following terms:
>
> ---
>
> **Renewal Terms Offered**
>
> | Detail | Information |
> |---|---|
> | Property address | [Full Address, Unit #] |
> | Current lease end date | [Date] |
> | Renewal term offered | [12-month / 6-month / Month-to-month] |
> | New lease start date | [Date] |
> | New lease end date | [Date] |
> | Current monthly rent | $[Amount] |
> | Proposed monthly rent | $[Amount] |
> | Rent increase amount | $[Amount] ([X]%) |
> | New rent effective date | [Date] |
> | Security deposit | No change / Additional $[Amount] required |
> | Changes to lease terms | [None / List any changes] |
>
> ---
>
> **To accept this renewal offer:**
> Please sign and return the enclosed renewal agreement by
> **[Response Deadline — typically 14–21 days from this notice].**
>
> You may return the signed agreement by:
> - Email to [Email Address]
> - [Tenant portal link if applicable]
> - In person at [Office Address if applicable]
>
> **If you choose not to renew:**
> Please provide written notice of your intent to vacate by
> [Notice Deadline]. Per your lease agreement, [X days] written notice
> is required. Your move-out date would be [Lease End Date].
>
> **If we do not receive a response by [Response Deadline]:**
> We will follow up directly. Failure to respond may affect your tenancy
> status and could result in the unit being listed for re-rental.
>
> Please do not hesitate to reach out if you have any questions about the
> renewal terms or would like to discuss further.
>
> Sincerely,  
> [Your Name]  
> [Title]  
> [Company Name] — Property Management  
> [Phone] | [Email]

## Template 3 — Follow-Up for Non-Responsive Tenant (30 Days Out)

**Purpose:** Tenant has not responded to Template 1 or Template 2.
Escalate urgency without being aggressive.

**Channel:** Email + text message  
**Tone:** Firm and professional

---

**Subject:** Follow-Up Required — Lease Renewal Decision — [Unit Address]

> Dear [Tenant Full Name],
>
> This is a follow-up to our previous messages regarding your lease renewal
> at [Full Property Address, Unit #], which expires on [Lease End Date] —
> now approximately 30 days away.
>
> We have not yet received a response confirming whether you intend to
> renew your lease or vacate the unit.
>
> **We need your decision by [Firm Deadline Date].**
>
> Your options at this time are:
>
> 1. **Renew your lease** — Sign and return the renewal agreement we sent
>    on [Date of Template 2] by [Deadline].
>
> 2. **Provide notice to vacate** — Submit written notice of your intent
>    to move out by [Notice Deadline] in accordance with your lease.
>
> 3. **Contact us to discuss** — If you have questions or concerns about
>    the renewal terms, please reach out today so we can address them.
>
> If we do not receive a response by [Firm Deadline], we will assume you
> do not intend to renew and will begin preparations to list the unit for
> re-rental. This may affect your options going forward.
>
> Please respond to this message or call us at [Phone Number] today.
>
> Sincerely,  
> [Your Name]  
> [Title]  
> [Company Name] — Property Management  
> [Phone] | [Email]

---

**Follow-up text message (same day as email above):**

> Hi [Tenant First Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company Name].
> We sent a follow-up email today regarding your lease renewal at
> [Address] — expiring [Date]. Please respond by [Deadline] with your
> decision. Call or text us at [Phone] with any questions. Thank you.

## Template 4 — Non-Renewal Notice to Tenant (30 Days Out)

**Purpose:** Management has decided not to offer renewal. Tenant must be
formally notified within the legally required notice period.

**Channel:** Email + certified mail (check state law)  
**Tone:** Formal, factual, respectful

> **Legal note:** Non-renewal notice requirements vary significantly by
> state and city. Some jurisdictions require just cause for non-renewal.
> Confirm requirements before sending. This template does not constitute
> a legal notice of termination.

---

**Subject:** Notice of Non-Renewal of Lease — [Unit Address]

> Dear [Tenant Full Name],
>
> This letter serves as formal written notice that your lease at
> [Full Property Address, Unit #], currently scheduled to expire on
> [Lease End Date], will not be renewed.
>
> Your tenancy will end on [Lease End Date]. You are required to vacate
> the unit and return all keys and access items by [Move-Out Date and
> Time — typically by noon on the last day].
>
> **Move-out expectations:**
> - The unit must be returned in clean condition, free of all personal
>   belongings and trash
> - All keys, garage remotes, parking passes, and access fobs must be
>   returned at time of move-out
> - A move-out inspection will be scheduled — you will be notified of the
>   date and time in advance
> - Your security deposit of $[Amount] will be processed in accordance
>   with [State] law and returned with an itemized statement within
>   [X days] of your move-out date
>
> If you have questions about your move-out obligations or wish to discuss
> this notice, please contact us at [Phone] or [Email].
>
> We appreciate your tenancy and wish you well in your next home.
>
> Sincerely,  
> [Your Name]  
> [Title]  
> [Company Name] — Property Management  
> [Phone] | [Email]

## Template 5 — Final Notice to Non-Responsive Tenant (14 Days Out)

**Purpose:** Tenant has not responded to any prior outreach. Final written
communication before escalation to owner or legal counsel.

**Channel:** Email with read receipt + certified mail  
**CC:** Property owner  
**Tone:** Formal, documented, no ambiguity

---

**Subject:** Final Notice — Lease Expiration — Immediate Response Required
— [Unit Address]

> Dear [Tenant Full Name],
>
> Despite multiple attempts to contact you regarding your lease at
> [Full Property Address, Unit #] — expiring on [Lease End Date] — we
> have not received any response.
>
> **Contact attempts on record:**
> - [Date] — [Method: Email / Text / Phone]
> - [Date] — [Method: Email / Text / Phone]
> - [Date] — [Method: Email / Text / Phone]
>
> Your lease expires in [X] days on [Lease End Date]. You are required
> to either:
>
> 1. Sign and return the renewal agreement immediately, or
> 2. Confirm your intent to vacate and provide a move-out date
>
> Failure to respond or vacate by [Lease End Date] may result in your
> occupancy being classified as a holdover tenancy, which carries
> additional legal and financial implications under [State] law.
>
> **Contact us immediately at [Phone] or [Email].**
>
> This communication is being retained in your tenant file and has been
> shared with the property owner.
>
> Sincerely,  
> [Your Name]  
> [Title]  
> [Company Name] — Property Management  
> [Phone] | [Email]
>
> cc: [Property Owner Name]

## Rent Increase Notice — Standalone Template

Use when issuing a rent increase notice separate from a full renewal offer,
or when required by state law as a standalone document.

> **State law reminder:** Many states and cities require specific advance
> notice periods for rent increases — commonly 30, 60, or 90 days.
> Some jurisdictions cap the percentage of allowable increases. Verify
> local requirements before issuing.

---

**Subject:** Notice of Rent Increase — [Unit Address] — Effective [Date]

> Dear [Tenant Full Name],
>
> This notice is to inform you that the monthly rent for your unit at
> [Full Property Address, Unit #] will increase as follows:
>
> | Detail | Amount |
> |---|---|
> | Current monthly rent | $[Current Amount] |
> | New monthly rent | $[New Amount] |
> | Increase amount | $[Difference] ([X]%) |
> | Effective date | [Date] |
>
> This notice is provided [X] days in advance in accordance with your
> lease agreement and applicable [State] law.
>
> Your first payment at the new rate of $[New Amount] will be due on
> [First Payment Date].
>
> All other terms and conditions of your existing lease agreement remain
> unchanged.
>
> If you have any questions regarding this notice, please contact us at
> [Phone] or [Email].
>
> Sincerely,  
> [Your Name]  
> [Title]  
> [Company Name] — Property Management  
> [Phone] | [Email]

## Dos and Don'ts

**Do:**
- Start the renewal process at least 60–90 days before lease expiration
- Always communicate in writing and retain copies of everything
- Confirm state-required notice periods before sending any formal notice
- Log every contact attempt with date, method, and outcome
- Notify the property owner before sending non-renewal notices
- Offer renewal terms that reflect current market conditions

**Do not:**
- Wait until 30 days out to begin the renewal conversation
- Send a rent increase notice without verifying local notice requirements
- Accept verbal renewal commitments — always get a signed agreement
- Ignore non-responsive tenants — document and escalate on schedule
- Promise renewal terms verbally that differ from what is in the written offer
- Skip the internal review — renewing on auto-pilot without checking
  payment history or market rent is a financial risk

# Vendor Dispatch and Follow-Up SOP

**Document type:** Standard Operating Procedure  
**Category:** Maintenance Operations — Vendor Management  
**Applies to:** Property managers, operations coordinators, maintenance staff  
**Last updated:** April 2026  
**Author:** Vicky | Property Management Operations

---

## Purpose

This SOP defines the end-to-end process for selecting, dispatching, tracking,
and closing out vendor work orders in a residential property management
operation. It ensures vendors are properly vetted, tenants are kept informed,
work is completed to standard, and invoices are verified before payment is
released.

Vendor coordination is one of the highest-risk areas in property management.
A weak vendor process leads to incomplete repairs, inflated invoices, tenant
complaints, and owner dissatisfaction. This SOP eliminates ambiguity at
every step.

---

## Scope

Applies to all third-party vendors and contractors dispatched to managed
properties, including but not limited to:

- Plumbers
- Electricians
- HVAC technicians
- General handymen
- Pest control operators
- Landscapers and lawn care
- Cleaning and turnover crews
- Roofers and structural contractors
- Appliance repair technicians
- Locksmiths

---

## Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Vendor** | Any third-party contractor or service provider dispatched to a property |
| **Preferred Vendor** | A pre-vetted vendor on the approved vendor list with confirmed insurance and licensing |
| **Work Order** | A logged and tracked maintenance request assigned to a vendor |
| **NTE Amount** | Not-to-Exceed — the maximum approved spend for a repair without additional owner approval |
| **Callback** | A return visit required because the original repair was incomplete or ineffective |
| **Invoice Verification** | The process of confirming that billed work matches the completed scope |

---

## Pre-Dispatch Requirements — Vendor Vetting

Never dispatch a vendor who has not been verified. Use only vendors on the
approved vendor list unless an emergency requires otherwise.

**Every approved vendor must have on file:**

- [ ] Valid business license (confirm annually)
- [ ] General liability insurance certificate — minimum $1M per occurrence
- [ ] Workers' compensation insurance (required if vendor has employees)
- [ ] W-9 form on file for tax reporting
- [ ] Signed vendor agreement or scope of work policy
- [ ] At least 2 verified references or prior work history with the company
- [ ] Emergency contact number

**For licensed trades (plumbing, electrical, HVAC):**
- [ ] Active state contractor license confirmed
- [ ] License number recorded in vendor file

> **Emergency exception:** If no approved vendor is available for an
> emergency repair, document why, obtain insurance proof before dispatch
> if at all possible, and add the vendor to the vetting queue immediately
> after the emergency is resolved.

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1 — Receive and classify the work order

Before dispatching any vendor, confirm the following from the maintenance
request:

- [ ] Issue clearly described and categorized by trade
- [ ] Priority level assigned (Emergency / Urgent / Routine)
- [ ] Unit address and access instructions confirmed
- [ ] Tenant contact and availability noted
- [ ] Photos of the issue received (request if not provided)
- [ ] Owner NTE threshold confirmed for this property

> Refer to the **Maintenance Request Handling SOP** for intake and
> classification procedures.

---

### Step 2 — Select the vendor

**First:** Check the approved vendor list for the correct trade.

**Selection criteria (in order):**
1. Availability within the required response window
2. Prior satisfactory work at this property or portfolio
3. Proximity to the property (faster response, lower trip charge)
4. Cost estimate within NTE threshold
5. Specialty licensing required for the job

**If multiple vendors are available for a routine repair:**
- Obtain 2–3 quotes for any job estimated above $500
- Document all quotes received before selecting

**If no approved vendor is available:**
- Escalate to supervisor or property owner immediately
- Document the gap in vendor coverage for that trade

---

### Step 3 — Confirm scope and estimate before dispatch

Before scheduling, confirm with the vendor:

- [ ] Vendor understands the full scope of the issue
- [ ] Vendor can complete the work within the required timeframe
- [ ] Estimated cost provided and within NTE threshold
- [ ] If estimate exceeds NTE — stop and obtain owner approval before proceeding

**Owner approval required when:**
- Repair estimate exceeds the pre-approved NTE (typically $300–$500 unless
  otherwise specified per property)
- Repair requires permits
- Repair involves structural, electrical panel, or roof work
- Repair is related to an active insurance claim

**Document owner approval:**
- Record date, time, method of approval, and amount approved
- Email confirmation from owner is preferred — follow up verbal approvals
  in writing immediately

---

### Step 4 — Schedule and dispatch

Once scope, cost, and approval are confirmed:

1. Schedule the appointment with the vendor
2. Confirm the exact date and arrival window (e.g., Tuesday, 10am–12pm)
3. Notify the tenant with required notice (minimum 24 hours except emergencies)
4. Provide vendor with:
   - Full property address and unit number
   - Tenant name and contact number
   - Access instructions (lockbox code, key pickup, tenant let-in)
   - Scope of work in writing
   - Any building or HOA access requirements
   - Pet or safety notes from tenant file
5. Update the work order with:
   - Vendor name and contact
   - Scheduled date and arrival window
   - Status: In Progress

**Tenant dispatch notification template — text:**

> Hi [Tenant Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company Name]. We have
> scheduled [Vendor / Trade — e.g., a plumber] to visit your unit at
> [Address] on [Date] between [Time Window]. They will [brief description
> of work — e.g., inspect and repair the kitchen faucet]. Please ensure
> access is available or contact us if this time does not work.
> Thank you.

---

### Step 5 — Day-of dispatch confirmation

On the day of the scheduled visit:

**Morning of appointment:**
- [ ] Confirm with vendor they are still scheduled and have all access info
- [ ] Send tenant a reminder if the appointment window is that day

**During appointment window:**
- [ ] Check in with vendor midway if no update received
- [ ] If vendor is running late, notify tenant immediately

**If vendor does not show:**
- Contact vendor immediately for explanation
- Notify tenant of the delay and revised timeline
- Document the no-show in the vendor's file — two no-shows triggers
  removal from preferred vendor list
- Reschedule within 24 hours

---

### Step 6 — Post-repair verification

Do not close a work order based on a vendor's word alone. Verify completion
through at least one of the following methods:

**Primary — tenant confirmation:**
Contact the tenant after the repair is complete and ask:
- Was the vendor professional and on time?
- Is the issue fully resolved?
- Was the unit left clean and undamaged?

**Secondary — vendor documentation:**
Request from vendor before payment is released:
- [ ] Before and after photos of the repair
- [ ] Written description of work completed
- [ ] Parts replaced or materials used (with quantities)
- [ ] Any follow-up work recommended

**If the repair is incomplete or the issue recurs within 30 days:**
- Contact the vendor to schedule a callback at no additional charge
- Document the callback in the work order
- If vendor refuses a callback for workmanship issues, escalate and flag
  for removal from vendor list

---

### Step 7 — Invoice verification

Never approve a vendor invoice without verifying it against the work order.

**Invoice verification checklist:**

- [ ] Vendor name matches the dispatched vendor
- [ ] Property address matches the work order
- [ ] Date of service matches the scheduled visit
- [ ] Scope of work on invoice matches what was authorized
- [ ] Line items are itemized (labor, parts, materials — not lump sum)
- [ ] Total amount is within the approved NTE or owner-approved amount
- [ ] No unauthorized charges or unapproved add-on work included

**If invoice discrepancies are found:**
1. Contact vendor in writing to request a corrected invoice
2. Do not approve payment until discrepancy is resolved
3. Document all communication in the work order

**Lump sum invoices:**
Request an itemized breakdown before approving any lump sum invoice
above $200. Non-itemized invoices make it impossible to verify fair
pricing and create audit risk.

---

### Step 8 — Submit invoice for payment

Once the invoice is verified and the work is confirmed complete:

- [ ] Attach verified invoice to the work order
- [ ] Attach before and after photos
- [ ] Attach tenant confirmation (text, email, or call log)
- [ ] Submit to accounting or owner for payment per your process
- [ ] Note payment submitted date in work order
- [ ] Update work order status: Pending Payment

---

### Step 9 — Close the work order

Work order is eligible to close when all of the following are complete:

- [ ] Repair physically completed and verified
- [ ] Tenant confirmed issue is resolved
- [ ] Invoice verified and submitted for payment
- [ ] All documentation attached to work order
- [ ] Vendor performance noted (satisfactory / callback required / do not use)
- [ ] Work order status updated: Closed
- [ ] Closed date recorded

> - Expired license or lapsed insurance

---

## Escalation Triggers

Escalate to property owner or supervisor immediately if:

- Repair estimate exceeds the NTE threshold
- Vendor identifies damage requiring permits or structural assessment
- Repair is related to a potential insurance claim
- Tenant reports vendor behaved inappropriately or caused damage
- No approved vendor is available within the emergency response window
- A callback has failed to resolve the original issue

## Response Time Standards by Priority

| Priority | Vendor Contact | Dispatch | On-Site Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| Emergency | Within 1 hour | Immediate | Within 2–4 hours |
| Urgent | Within 4 hours | Within 24 hours | Within 24–48 hours |
| Routine | Within 1 business day | Within 3 business days | Within 5–7 business days |

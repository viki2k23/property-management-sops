# Maintenance Request Handling SOP

**Document type:** Standard Operating Procedure  
**Category:** Maintenance Operations  
**Applies to:** Remote property managers, operations coordinators, leasing support staff  
**Last updated:** April 2026  
**Author:** Vicky | Property Management Operations  

---

## Purpose

This SOP establishes a consistent, repeatable process for receiving, triaging,
coordinating, and closing tenant maintenance requests. It is designed to reduce
response time, improve tenant satisfaction, protect property condition, and
create a clear audit trail for all maintenance activity.

---

## Scope

This procedure applies to all maintenance requests submitted by tenants across
managed residential units, including single-family homes, multi-unit buildings,
and apartment portfolios.

---

## Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Work Order** | A logged maintenance request assigned a tracking number |
| **Emergency Repair** | A condition posing immediate risk to health, safety, or property integrity |
| **Routine Repair** | A non-urgent issue that does not affect habitability |
| **Vendor** | Licensed contractor or service provider dispatched to complete a repair |
| **Habitability** | The legal standard requiring rental units to be safe and livable |
| **ETA** | Estimated time of arrival communicated to tenant |

---

## Emergency vs. Routine Classification

Correctly classifying the request at intake determines response time and
escalation path.

### Emergency — respond within 1 hour

- No heat in winter (below 55°F inside unit)
- No hot water
- Active water leak or flooding
- Gas leak (instruct tenant to evacuate and call 911 immediately)
- Sewage backup
- No electricity affecting the entire unit
- Broken exterior door lock or window (security risk)
- Fire damage (call 911 first)

### Urgent — respond within 24 hours

- Refrigerator not cooling (food spoilage risk)
- HVAC not functioning in extreme heat
- Partial electrical outage (single outlet or room)
- Pest infestation (rodents, cockroaches, bedbugs)
- Clogged toilet (only toilet in unit)
- Broken water heater

### Routine — respond within 3–5 business days

- Dripping faucet
- Broken cabinet hinge or door handle
- Minor drywall damage
- Non-emergency appliance issue
- Caulking or weatherstripping
- Light fixture replacement
- Garbage disposal not working

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1 — Receive the request

**Accepted intake channels:**
- Tenant portal (preferred)
- Email
- Text message
- Phone call (document immediately)

**Capture at intake:**
- Tenant full name
- Unit address and unit number
- Date and time of request
- Description of the issue in tenant's own words
- Photos or videos (request if not provided)
- Tenant's preferred contact method
- Tenant's availability for access

> **Note:** If a tenant calls by phone, confirm all details verbally and
> follow up with a written summary via email or text for documentation.

---

### Step 2 — Log the work order

Create a work order entry in your property management system
(e.g., AppFolio, Buildium, Propertyware, or a shared tracking sheet)
immediately upon receiving the request.

**Work order must include:**
- Unique tracking number
- Unit address
- Tenant name and contact
- Issue category (plumbing, electrical, HVAC, appliance, structural, pest, other)
- Priority level (Emergency / Urgent / Routine)
- Date and time received
- Assigned staff or vendor (once determined)
- Status (Open / In Progress / Pending Tenant / Completed / Closed)

---

### Step 3 — Acknowledge the tenant

Send acknowledgment within the timeframes below:

| Priority | Acknowledgment deadline |
|---|---|
| Emergency | Within 30 minutes |
| Urgent | Within 4 hours |
| Routine | Within 1 business day |

**Acknowledgment must include:**
- Confirmation that the request was received
- Estimated next step (vendor contact, inspection scheduling, or direct repair)
- Name of the point of contact handling their request

**Template — routine acknowledgment:**

> Hi [Tenant Name],
>
> Thank you for reporting this. We have logged your maintenance request for
> [brief issue description] at [unit address] and assigned it to our team.
> We will follow up within [X business days] with next steps.
>
> Please do not hesitate to reach out if the issue worsens or becomes urgent.
>
> [Your Name]  
> [Company Name] — Property Management

---

### Step 4 — Triage and assign

**If the issue is an emergency:**
1. Contact the emergency vendor or on-call contractor immediately by phone
2. Confirm ETA and relay to tenant within 30 minutes
3. Notify property owner if repair is expected to exceed $500 (or per owner threshold)
4. Document all calls with timestamps

**If the issue is urgent or routine:**
1. Review the preferred vendor list for the appropriate trade
2. Contact vendor to confirm availability and estimated cost
3. If cost exceeds the pre-approved repair threshold, obtain owner approval before scheduling
4. Schedule the repair and confirm the appointment date and time with the tenant

**Vendor dispatch checklist:**
- [ ] Vendor confirmed for correct trade (plumber, electrician, HVAC tech, etc.)
- [ ] Vendor provided full unit address and access instructions
- [ ] Appointment date and time confirmed
- [ ] Tenant notified of scheduled visit
- [ ] Work order updated with vendor name and scheduled date

---

### Step 5 — Coordinate access

**Standard access notice:** Provide tenant a minimum of 24 hours notice before
entry, except in emergencies where immediate access is required.

**Access options:**
- Tenant present during repair
- Lockbox code provided to vendor (document code shared and revoke after use)
- Property manager present for access

**Confirm with vendor before dispatch:**
- Entry instructions (lockbox, key, tenant-let-in)
- Parking availability
- Any HOA or building access requirements
- Pet or safety considerations noted by tenant

---

### Step 6 — Follow up with vendor

On the scheduled repair date, confirm by text or call:
- Vendor is en route or on-site
- Repair is completed or if additional parts or visits are needed

**If the vendor cannot complete the repair:**
- Document the reason
- Notify the tenant immediately
- Reschedule within 24 hours
- Update the work order status

---

### Step 7 — Notify tenant of completion

Once the repair is confirmed complete by the vendor:

1. Contact the tenant to confirm the issue has been resolved
2. Ask if the repair is satisfactory and if any follow-up is needed
3. Send a written close-out confirmation

**Template — repair completion notice:**

> Hi [Tenant Name],
>
> We wanted to follow up regarding the recent maintenance work completed at
> your unit on [date]. Our vendor [Vendor Name / Company] completed the repair
> for [brief description of issue].
>
> Please let us know if the issue has been fully resolved or if there is
> anything that still requires attention.
>
> Thank you for your patience.
>
> [Your Name]  
> [Company Name] — Property Management

---

### Step 8 — Close the work order

Close the work order only when all of the following are confirmed:

- [ ] Repair physically completed by vendor
- [ ] Tenant confirmed the issue is resolved
- [ ] Invoice received from vendor
- [ ] Invoice approved and submitted for payment
- [ ] All photos, notes, and communications attached to the work order
- [ ] Work order status updated to "Closed" with date

> **Do not close a work order based on vendor confirmation alone.
> Tenant confirmation is required.**

---

## Escalation Triggers

Escalate to the property owner immediately if any of the following occur:

- Estimated repair cost exceeds the pre-approved threshold (typically $300–$500)
- Tenant reports a habitability issue (no heat, no water, sewage backup)
- Repair requires permits or licensed contractor certification
- Tenant threatens legal action or references tenant rights
- A vendor cannot be sourced within the required response window
- Structural damage is identified during the repair

## Response Time Standards Summary

| Priority | Acknowledge | Dispatch | Resolution target |
|---|---|---|---|
| Emergency | 30 minutes | Immediate | Same day |
| Urgent | 4 hours | Within 24 hours | Within 48–72 hours |
| Routine | 1 business day | Within 3 business days | Within 5–7 business days |

---

## Record-Keeping Requirements

All maintenance records must be retained for a minimum of 3 years, including:

- Original work order with timestamps
- All tenant communications related to the request
- Vendor invoices and receipts
- Photos before and after the repair
- Owner approval documentation (if applicable)
- Close-out confirmation from tenant
